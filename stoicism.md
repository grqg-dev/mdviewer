# Stoicism

Stoicism is a school of ancient Greek philosophy founded in Athens by Zeno of Citium around 300 BCE. Its central teaching: the only true good is virtue, and the only true evil is vice. Everything else — wealth, health, reputation, death — is "indifferent."

## Core Ideas

- **The dichotomy of control** — Some things are up to us (judgments, intentions, desires), most things are not (body, reputation, outcomes). Focus only on what you control.
- **Virtue is sufficient for happiness** — A good life requires only a good character. Fortune cannot take that from you.
- **Live according to nature** — Human nature is rational and social. To live well is to live rationally and in community with others.
- **The view from above** — Zoom out. Most things that feel urgent are trivial at cosmic scale.

## The Four Virtues

| Virtue | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Wisdom | Knowing what is good, bad, and indifferent |
| Justice | Treating others fairly; fulfilling social duties |
| Courage | Acting rightly despite fear or difficulty |
| Temperance | Self-discipline; not being ruled by appetite |

## Key Figures

- **Zeno of Citium** — founder; taught on the *Stoa Poikilē* (painted porch), giving Stoicism its name
- **Epictetus** — freed slave; his *Enchiridion* is the most direct Stoic manual
- **Marcus Aurelius** — Roman Emperor; his *Meditations* were private notes never meant for publication
- **Seneca** — statesman and writer; *Letters to Lucilius* is among the most readable Stoic texts
